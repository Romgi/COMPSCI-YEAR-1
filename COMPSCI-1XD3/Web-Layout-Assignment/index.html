Headline One: City Council Approves Late-Night Transit Trial

After months of debate, the city will run a three-month pilot extending bus service past midnight on key routes. Officials say the goal is safer commutes for late shifts and students. Riders can expect more frequent service on weekends. The transit agency will publish on-time and safety reports every two weeks.

A university team announced a lab prototype that charges more quickly while keeping temperatures low. The researchers say the design could reduce charging time for small devices and e-bikes. Headline Six: Museum Announces Free Admission Weekend

The museum will offer free entry and short guided tours as part of a new outreach initiative. Organizers say the goal is to make exhibits more accessible to families. Several local artists will host drop-in demonstrations. Tickets are first-come, first-served at the door.

Headline Seven: Small Business Grant Program Opens Applications

The city launched a new grant for storefront improvements in designated corridors. Applicants must submit a simple budget and timeline. Priority will go to accessibility upgrades and energy-efficient retrofits. The deadline is in four weeks.
